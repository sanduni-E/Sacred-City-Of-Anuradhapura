Location

Transportation Options

Anuradhapura is served by railway and highways. The Northern railway line connects Anuradhapura with Colombo, Jaffna, and Kankesanthurai. Anuradhapura railway station is the city's rail gateway, with major services, such as the Yal Devi, stopping there.

Anuradhapura is a central city in Sri Lanka. It is directly connected by road to a large number of major cities and towns on the island. By road, it is connected to Vavuniya, Dambulla, Matale, Puttalam, Trincomalee, Jaffna, Kurunegala and Kandy.

Healthcare Facilities

Anuradhapura Teaching Hospital, the only tertiary care hospital in the North Central region and one of the largest in Sri Lanka, offers healthcare services to a population of over two million within the district and in neighbouring districts. With a daily average of over 1,000 out patients and an occupancy rate of 115% the hospital was congested and therefore hindered the provision of appropriate and timely medical services.

The new facility and equipment were provided at the request of the Government of Sri Lanka to facilitate the transformation of the Anuradhapura hospital from a general hospital to a teaching hospital. The hospital with its new facility stands as a beacon of hope to thousands of people across the district and in neighbouring districts, providing quality healthcare services and minimizing difficulties faced by patients who will otherwise have to travel long distances to obtain such services.

Education System

Most of rural and popular schools are situated in anuradhapura province.As categories Private schools(Sussex College,Lyceum International College,Cambridge International College),Kannangara Central Colleges(Anuradhapura Cental College,Kekirawa Central College) which provides secondary education,six National Schools,seven Provincial Schools,20 of Top schools and seven top Primary Schools.

Considering higher education there are Rajarata University of Sri Lanka (that include Medical/Applied Sciences/Management/Art/Agriculture faculties),Open University of Sri Lanka(major branch that include all diplomas and degrees) and University College of Anuradhapura.

Climate

The climate here is tropical. The summers are much rainier than the winters in Anuradhapura. This location is classified as Aw by Köppen and Geiger. In Anuradhapura, the average annual temperature is 27.3 °C. In a year, the average rainfall is 1368 mm.

The driest month is June, with 12 mm of rain. The greatest amount of precipitation occurs in November, with an average of 249 mm.

May is the warmest month of the year. The temperature in May averages 28.7 °C. The lowest average temperatures in the year occur in January, when it is around 24.7 °C.
